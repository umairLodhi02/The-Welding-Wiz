Non-destructive testing

NDT is a technique employed by industries to assess the properties of materials,
components, structures, or systems for characteristic variations, welding defects, and
discontinuities, all without damaging the original part.

Magnetic particle inspection

Magnetic Particle Inspection is a reliable technique used to detect surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials. By applying magnetic fields and iron particles, cracks, seams, or other discontinuities become visible. This method is particularly effective for identifying flaws that could compromise the integrity of welds and structures. It is quick, cost-effective, and widely used in the welding and construction industries.

Ultrasonic particle inspection

Ultrasonic Testing utilizes high-frequency sound waves to detect internal flaws like cracks, voids, and inclusions within materials. This non-destructive method provides highly accurate results, making it ideal for evaluating weld quality and ensuring structural integrity. Its ability to penetrate deep into materials makes it a preferred choice for critical inspections in industries like aerospace and manufacturing.

Dyepenetrant

Dye Penetrant Testing is a simple yet effective method for detecting surface defects in materials. A liquid dye is applied to the surface, which seeps into any cracks or openings, making them visible under inspection. Suitable for both ferrous and non-ferrous materials, this method is widely used to identify imperfections that could lead to structural failure. It is easy to implement and provides reliable results for surface flaw detection.

Radiography

Radiographic Testing uses X-rays or gamma rays to examine the internal structure of welds and materials. By creating detailed radiographic images, this method can reveal hidden defects such as porosity, inclusions, or cracks. It is a highly reliable technique, widely employed in industries requiring stringent quality control, such as oil and gas or shipbuilding.

Eddycurrent

Eddy Current Testing employs electromagnetic fields to identify surface and subsurface defects in conductive materials. This non-contact method is fast, efficient, and versatile, making it suitable for inspecting various components, including welds and thin materials. It is widely used for detecting corrosion, cracks, or other discontinuities in industries like aviation and automotive.

Phase array

Phased Array Ultrasonics is an advanced form of ultrasonic testing that uses multiple sensors to produce detailed cross-sectional images of materials. This method provides exceptional accuracy in detecting and sizing defects, even in complex geometries. Its versatility and precision make it ideal for inspecting critical welds and ensuring high safety standards in industries like power generation and pipelines.
